0 / The Cost of Documentation Failure

Documentation errors cost importers far more than they realise.

The physical product is almost never the problem. It is the paperwork, and the consequences compound from the port all the way to the supermarket shelf. Click any card for detail.

🏛️
Port Detention
€200-€800/day from the moment a shipment is held. Standard cargo insurance does not cover it.
Click to expand
  • Storage charges accumulate from the first day of hold
  • Physical examination fees charged regardless of outcome
  • Re-export costs often exceed original freight cost
  • Destruction costs where re-export is not permitted (perishables, contamination risk)
  • Most standard ocean cargo policies explicitly exclude detention and demurrage
📋
Regulatory Designation
One failure triggers automatic inspection on every future shipment. A permanent compounding cost.
Click to expand
  • FDA DWPE: Detention Without Physical Examination applied to repeat offenders
  • EU border control systems flag importers and exporters independently
  • All subsequent shipments face mandatory physical inspection
  • Clearance times increase across all products, not just the offending one
  • Blacklisting of specific exporters cuts off sourcing entirely
⚠️
Retail Recall
Errors that clear the border trigger recalls from the shelf. Average cost: €500K-€10M.
Click to expand
  • Mislabelled allergens, non-conforming origin marking, fraudulent certifications
  • Physical retrieval from every retail and distribution node; cost scales with penetration
  • Retailer penalty clauses: punitive and non-negotiable
  • RASFF notification (EU) or FDA recall classification (US) triggers formal investigation
  • Class action exposure in the US for allergen or health claim errors
  • Retailer delisting: one recall is frequently sufficient grounds
🔗
Supply Chain Disruption
Rejected shipments break production schedules, replenishment cycles, and buyer commitments.
Click to expand
  • Emergency sourcing at premium cost and inferior terms
  • Contract penalty clauses triggered by missed delivery windows
  • Retail listing at risk where out-of-stock periods exceed tolerance
  • Capital tied up in detained shipment unavailable for next purchase cycle
⚖️
Legal Liability
The importer of record carries full legal responsibility. Allergen errors carry class action risk in the US.
Click to expand
  • EU and US: importer of record liable for compliance regardless of exporter fault
  • Licence and permit revocation for organic, protected designation, or quota products
  • Criminal liability possible where fraud is demonstrated
  • Insurance voidance where errors are deemed preventable administrative failures

1 / Channel Rationale

Freight forwarders are the distribution layer that already exists.

VeriPura does not need to build a sales force to reach importers. It needs to sign freight forwarders. Every FF carries an importer book. Every importer book is a distribution network.

They sit at the compliance intersection
FFs coordinate documents, manage customs clearance, and carry existing importer trust. The natural point to embed compliance verification as a service line.
They cannot build this themselves
FFs are logistics operators, not compliance technology vendors. VeriPura fills a gap they cannot close independently, at no operational cost to them.
One FF unlocks an entire importer network
Signing one FF activates their entire client base across multiple corridors simultaneously. Signing FFs is the unit of growth, not individual importers.
Adjacent channels compound distribution
Customs brokers, port health agents, and marine cargo insurers share the same document flow. Each amplifies FF adoption without requiring separate sales infrastructure.

Four tiers. One document flow. Compounding distribution.

Tier 4 / Demand Driver

  • Does not distribute VeriPura directly
  • Creates pull-through adoption via premium reductions for verified importers
  • Functions as third-party validator of VeriPura's risk-reduction efficacy
  • Approached after pilot impact is established

The white-label early engagement advantage. The FF that offers VeriPura (white-labeled as their own compliance product) presents themselves to the importer at Step 1, not Step 4. For importers with contracts with multiple FFs, VeriPura becomes the differentiating reason to consolidate volume with the FF who provides it.

6 / Importer Billing

Billed on completion. Never on submission.

📨
Referral model: VeriPura invoices the importer directly
Per shipment, post-verification. FF receives their commission separately on a monthly consolidated statement.
🏷️
White label model: the FF invoices the importer
VeriPura invoices the FF monthly at the wholesale rate. The importer sees only the FF's brand and pricing.
⚡
Billing trigger: issuance of the Findings Report only
Never on submission. The importer's cost is tied directly to a deliverable they already need.
🛡️
Below-threshold shipments: reduced processing floor
Where confidence threshold is not met and no attestation is issued, the fee reduces to approximately 30% of the base rate. Protects importer trust.
7 / Adjacent Channel Integration

Three adjacent channels. Each one amplifies FF adoption.

Customs Brokers

Channel structure

  • Identical channel architecture to FF; same two-model margin structure
  • FF and customs broker in the same entity: enhanced volume pricing
  • Independent customs brokers: signed as Direct Channel Partners

Strategic value

  • Significant overlap with FF function in smaller and emerging markets
  • Often hold independent importer relationships FFs do not reach
  • A single customs broker signing opens an entirely distinct importer segment

Port Health Agents and Authorities

Role in the channel

  • Not a revenue channel; a legitimacy channel
  • Pilots with port health bodies (e.g. Suffolk) generate reference cases
  • Institutional endorsement de-risks FF adoption conversations

Future integration

  • Port health pre-clearance flag could be fed into VeriPura's validation layer
  • A credibility asset in FF and investor conversations

Marine Cargo and Trade Insurers

Current coverage

  • Standard marine cargo: 0.1%-0.5% of shipment value for basic cover
  • Specialty food: 0.5%-2% of shipment value
  • Comprehensive cover: up to 2.5% of annual revenue for mid-size importers
  • Covers physical loss, contamination, temperature excursion where endorsed

What standard insurance does not cover

  • Detention and demurrage: explicitly excluded by most policies
  • Commercial unsaleability from documentation failures: outside policy triggers
  • Losses from the importer's own documentation errors: excluded as preventable

The analogue is established. Usage-Based Insurance programs (pioneered by Progressive, now global) offer premium discounts to policyholders who demonstrate verifiable safe behaviour via telematics. VeriPura's on-chain attestations provide the same verified, auditable evidence base to a cargo underwriter. Supply chain verification and blockchain tracking are already recognised by specialist underwriters as premium-reducing factors.

VeriPura's approach: once the pilot programme establishes measurable impact on rejection rates, we will approach specialist marine cargo underwriters (Lloyd's syndicates, specialist food trade insurers) to formalise a premium discount framework. Insurer endorsement then becomes a third-party validation of VeriPura's risk-reduction efficacy, a credibility asset in every FF and importer sales conversation.
