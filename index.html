01 / The Problem

Documentation errors cost your clients far more than they realise.

39% of FDA food rejections in 2025 were caused by preventable paperwork errors. The consequences of those errors compound from the port all the way to the supermarket shelf.

🏛️
Port Detention
€200–€800/day in storage charges from the moment a shipment is held. Physical examination fees, re-export freight, or destruction costs follow. Exclusions in most cargo insurance policies mean the importer absorbs this alone.
📋
Regulatory Designation
FDA DWPE and EU border control systems flag repeat offenders for automatic physical inspection on every future shipment. One documentation failure becomes a permanent compounding cost across the entire import programme.
⚠️
Retail Recall
Documentation errors that clear the border : mislabelled allergens, non-conforming origin marking, fraudulent certifications, trigger recalls after the product reaches the shelf. Average cost: €500K–€10M. Existential for smaller importers.
🔗
Supply Chain Disruption
Rejected shipments break downstream production schedules, retail replenishment cycles, and buyer commitments. Emergency sourcing at premium cost. Contract penalties. Lost listings. All from an administrative failure, not a product failure.
⚖️
Legal Liability
The importer of record carries full legal responsibility for compliance of goods placed on the market. Allergen or health claim errors carry class action exposure in the US. Licence and permit revocation risk in the EU.
💡
The Core Irrationality
Most of these failures are caused by errors in paperwork, not problems with the product. The importer pays the full cost of a physical rejection for what is, at root, an administrative mistake. This is what VeriPura eliminates.

02 / The Solution

VeriPura validates compliance before the shipment moves.

We enter the workflow at the Purchase Order, not at the port. That is the only intervention point that eliminates document-related risk across all three tiers of the cost hierarchy simultaneously.

01
Importer issues Purchase Order to exporter
Specifying the product, origin, and destination regulatory regime (EU: TRACES NT / IPAFFS / BTOM; US: FDA FSMA 204; or any other configured jurisdiction).
02
VeriPura generates a structured document checklist VeriPura enters here
Specific to the HS code, country of origin, and destination regime. The exporter knows exactly what is required before they prepare a single document.
03
Exporter returns documents. VeriPura validates the full set
Completeness, accuracy, and legitimacy, including certifications (phytosanitary, health attestations, origin, organic, halal) that never pass through the freight forwarder. On-chain attestation issued where confidence threshold is met.
04
VeriPura issues a pre-shipment Findings Report
The importer has a structured, auditable compliance record before the shipment moves. Errors are corrected at source, not caught at the border.
05
Importer engages the freight forwarder
With a clean, VeriPura-attested document package already in hand. You receive shipments with materially lower rejection risk at customs.
06
FF manages customs clearance and onward logistics
As normal, with the documentation risk already eliminated upstream.
