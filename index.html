Six things every investor needs to understand about this strategy.

VeriPura's channel strategy is built on a single structural insight: freight forwarders already touch every shipment, already hold the importer relationship, and cannot build compliance technology themselves. VeriPura fills that gap and gives the FF a new service line in the process.

0 / The Cost of Documentation Failure

The importer pays a physical price for an administrative mistake.

Every food import shipment travels with a documentary chain. When any element is incomplete, inaccurate, or illegitimate, the importer bears the full consequences regardless of whether the physical product is compliant.

1 / Channel Rationale

Freight forwarders are the distribution layer that already exists.

VeriPura does not need to build a sales force to reach importers. It needs to sign freight forwarders. Every FF carries an importer book. Every importer book is a distribution network.

They sit at the compliance intersection
Freight forwarders coordinate documents, manage customs clearance, and carry existing importer trust. They are the natural point to embed compliance verification as a service line.
They cannot build this themselves
FFs are logistics operators, not compliance technology vendors. VeriPura fills a gap they cannot close independently, at no operational cost to them.
One FF unlocks an entire importer network
The FF relationship converts a cold sales problem (reaching importers one by one) into a warm network problem. Signing one FF activates their entire client base across multiple corridors simultaneously.
Adjacent channels compound distribution
Customs brokers, port health agents, and marine cargo insurers share the same document flow. Each acts as a referral node, legitimacy signal, or demand driver without requiring separate sales infrastructure.
2 / Channel Tiers

Four tiers. One document flow. Compounding distribution.

VeriPura's channel architecture is structured around the four parties that touch or influence a food import shipment's documentary chain.

  • Manages the importer relationship and invoice
  • Natural point to embed VeriPura as a service line
  • White label or referral commission model

  • May operate independently; separate sign-on required
  • High-value target where FF and customs broker are the same entity
  • Same two-model margin structure as FF tier

  • Does not bill the importer directly

Tier 4 / Demand Driver
Marine Cargo Insurer
  • Does not distribute VeriPura directly
  • Creates pull-through adoption by offering premium reductions to VeriPura-verified importers
  • Functions as a third-party validator of VeriPura's risk-reduction efficacy
  • Approached after pilot impact is established
3 / FF Channel Mechanics

VeriPura enters at Step 1. The FF currently enters at Step 4.

The conventional import workflow is where VeriPura finds its leverage. By entering at PO issuance rather than at customs filing, VeriPura eliminates errors at source and repositions the FF as a compliance partner from the first document.

4b. Dynamic Risk Pricing: the data moat

VeriPura aggregates rejection data from multiple regulatory regimes, anchored on EU border records (TRACES NT, RASFF) and US FDA refusal data (12,486 food rejections in 2025, across 287 charge codes), expanding to all corridors active on the platform. This multi-regime dataset is a proprietary asset that compounds with every shipment processed.

Exporter identity

Historical rejection rate for this specific exporter, across all destination regimes on the platform. Compounds as transaction volume grows.

Country of origin

Aggregate rejection rate by origin country for the relevant product category, per destination regime. The same origin can carry different risk profiles per destination.

HS code

Rejection frequency and charge code concentration for the specific product classification, per importing jurisdiction. Flat-fee tools cannot price this distinction.

This positions VeriPura's fee as actuarially grounded: the pricing logic of an insurer, not an arbitrary service charge. The moat widens with every shipment verified, across every corridor and regime on the platform.

6 / Importer Billing

Billed on completion. Never on submission.

VeriPura's billing model is designed to protect importer trust and align incentives: the fee is only triggered when a Findings Report is issued, not when a shipment is submitted for review.

📨
Referral model: VeriPura invoices the importer directly
Per shipment, post-verification. VeriPura manages the billing relationship. The FF receives their commission separately on a monthly consolidated statement.
🏷️
White label model: the FF invoices the importer
VeriPura invoices the FF on a consolidated monthly statement at the wholesale rate. The importer sees only the FF's brand and pricing. The FF owns the billing relationship.
⚡
Billing trigger: issuance of the Findings Report
Not submission. Only charged on completion of a verified Findings Report. The importer's cost is tied directly to a deliverable they already need.
🛡️
Below-threshold shipments: reduced processing floor
Where a Findings Report cannot be issued (confidence threshold not met, no attestation), the fee is reduced to a processing floor of approximately 30% of the base rate. This protects importer trust and VeriPura's commercial relationship.
7 / Adjacent Channel Integration

Three adjacent channels. Each one amplifies FF adoption.

Beyond the primary FF channel, VeriPura has three adjacent channels that create legitimacy, distribution, and demand pull without requiring separate sales infrastructure.

Customs Brokers

Channel structure

  • Treated identically to FFs in the channel architecture; same two-model margin structure applies
  • Where a single entity acts as both FF and customs broker, they qualify for enhanced volume pricing
  • Customs brokers without FF function are signed as Direct Channel Partners, not sub-admins of an FF

Strategic value

  • Significant overlap with FF function in smaller or emerging markets
  • Often hold independent importer relationships that FFs do not reach
  • A single customs broker signing can open an entirely distinct importer segment

Port Health Agents and Authorities

Role in the channel

  • Not a revenue channel; a legitimacy channel
  • Do not bill the importer directly
  • Pilots with port health bodies (e.g. Suffolk) generate reference cases that de-risk FF adoption conversations

Future integration potential

  • Port health pre-clearance flag could be fed into VeriPura's document validation layer
  • Institutional endorsement from a port health authority is a credibility asset in FF and investor conversations

Marine Cargo and Trade Insurers: Demand Driver

Current insurance landscape

  • Standard marine cargo insurance for food imports costs 0.1%-0.5% of shipment value for basic coverage, rising to 0.5%-2% for specialty food products
  • Comprehensive cover can reach 0.5%-2.5% of annual revenue for mid-size importers
  • Coverage typically includes physical loss, contamination, temperature excursion (where endorsed), and customs rejection as a named peril

What insurance does not cover

  • Detention and demurrage fees: explicitly excluded by most standard ocean cargo policies
  • Documentation failures that result in commercial unsaleability: outside standard policy triggers
  • Losses arising from the importer's own documentation errors: excluded categorically as preventable administrative failures
The UBI analogue. Usage-Based Insurance programs (pioneered by Progressive in the early 2000s, now global) offer premium discounts to policyholders who demonstrate verifiable safe behaviour. Studies show UBI programs reduce accident risk by approximately 50%. The structural parallel to VeriPura is direct: verified, auditable, shipment-level compliance data gives the insurer the evidence base to price documentation risk more accurately. In food cargo insurance specifically, supply chain verification and blockchain tracking are already recognised by specialist underwriters as premium-reducing factors.
VeriPura's approach. Once the pilot programme has established measurable impact on rejection rates and documentation quality, VeriPura will approach specialist marine cargo underwriters (Lloyd's syndicates and specialist food trade insurers) to establish a formal premium discount framework for VeriPura-verified importers. A 15-20% underwriter discount would recover 0.22-0.30% of shipment value in premium savings for the importer, narrowing the net compliance cost of VeriPura materially. Insurer endorsement then becomes a third-party validation of VeriPura's risk-reduction efficacy, a credibility asset in FF and importer sales conversations independent of any commercial arrangement.
